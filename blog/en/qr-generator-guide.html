Common QR Code Use Cases

Link Sharing: Convert URLs, article links, or video links into QR codes so users can access them instantly by scanning 鈥?no need to type long web addresses. This is especially useful on posters, billboards, and business cards.

Business Cards & Contact Info: Encode your name, phone number, email, and company details into a QR code. Recipients can scan and save your contact to their phone with a single tap.

WiFi Sharing: Generate a QR code containing your WiFi SSID and password. Guests scan the code to connect automatically 鈥?no need to share passwords verbally or write them down.

Payments: Mobile payment platforms widely use QR codes for transactions. Merchants display a payment code, and customers scan to complete purchases quickly and securely.

Event Check-in & Ticketing: Conferences, exhibitions, and performances use QR code e-tickets. Attendees scan for fast entry while organizers verify identities in real time.

QR Code Security Tips

While QR codes are convenient, they also carry security risks. Since QR codes are not human-readable, attackers can embed malicious links disguised as legitimate codes.

Never scan codes from unknown sources: Be cautious of QR codes posted in public places like streets, elevators, or ad boards. Only scan codes from trusted sources.

Watch for phishing links: Always check the URL you are redirected to after scanning. Cybercriminals often use fake login pages that mimic real websites to steal credentials.

Be careful with permissions: Some QR codes request login authorization or access to personal data. Decline such requests unless absolutely necessary. Verify the recipient's identity before authorizing any payment or data access.

Use secure scanning tools: Prefer your phone's built-in camera scanner or a reputable security app for scanning QR codes. These tools often include link safety checks and can block malicious URLs.

How to Generate High-Quality QR Codes

Generating high-quality QR codes improves the scanning experience and ensures reliable recognition across different environments.

Choose the right size: The QR code size should match its use case. For business cards, use at least 2脳2 cm. Posters and billboards need much larger sizes for long-distance scanning.

Set an appropriate error correction level: QR codes support four levels (L, M, Q, H). Higher levels allow successful scanning even when the code is partially damaged or obscured. Use level H for challenging environments.

Maintain sufficient quiet zone: Leave a margin of at least 4 module-widths around the QR code. This quiet zone helps scanners accurately detect the code boundaries.

Test scannability: After generating your QR code, test it with different phone models and scanning apps. Ensure it works at various distances and lighting conditions.

Equal Installment vs Equal Principal: Which to Choose?

Choosing between equal installment and equal principal repayment methods is one of the biggest financial decisions when taking out a mortgage. The difference can save you tens of thousands of dollars in interest.

Key Differences

Equal Installment: Fixed monthly payment throughout the loan term. Principal portion increases monthly while interest decreases. Best for stable monthly budgeting.

Equal Principal: Same principal amount each month, interest decreases over time. Total interest is lower, but early payments are higher.

Example Comparison ($500K, 30yr, 3.85%)

  • Equal Installment: Fixed ~$2,344/month, total interest ~$344K
  • Equal Principal: Starts at ~$2,993/month, decreasing, total interest ~$290K

The difference in total interest is about $54K. Equal principal saves money but requires higher early payments.

How to Choose

1. Income Stability

Stable salary earners → Equal Installment. Rising income → Equal Principal.

2. Prepayment Plans

Planning to prepay within 5-10 years? Equal Principal saves more. Paying full term? Equal Installment is simpler.

3. Monthly Budget

Keep mortgage under 30% of household income. If Equal Principal's first payment is stressful, choose Equal Installment.

4. Life Stage

Young professionals → Equal Principal. Mid-career with family expenses → Equal Installment. Near retirement → shorter loan term.
